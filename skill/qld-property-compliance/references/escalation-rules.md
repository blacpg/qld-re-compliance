# Escalation and Conflict Rules

## Authority conflict rules

1. **Commentary vs law.** Industry commentary that conflicts with legislation or regulation is set aside. Legislation/regulation wins.
2. **Guidance vs law.** If regulator guidance appears inconsistent with legislation, flag the conflict and do **not** resolve it as legal advice. Present both and recommend escalation.
3. **Internal policy stricter than law.** Phrase as "Blac policy requires X", never "the law requires X".
4. **Commentary alone is never mandatory.** A rule sourced only from `industry_commentary` cannot be stated as a "must".

## When to escalate to a human

Escalate whenever a matching rule has `human_escalation_required: true`. State the specific `escalation_reason`. Common reasons:

- possible commission recovery issue
- contract termination consequence
- seller disclosure defect
- trust account issue
- complaint or OFT risk

## How to escalate

Do not say a generic "see a solicitor". Say what the risk is and who to take it to:

> This touches a possible contract-termination consequence under the seller disclosure scheme. Before acting, confirm with a solicitor, because getting it wrong can give the buyer a right to terminate.

The point of escalation is to tell the agent *why* the stakes are high, so they take it seriously.
